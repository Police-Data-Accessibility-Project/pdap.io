Jobs at PDAP

Some context

Our project has been in motion since 2020, putting down roots in Pittsburgh as a non-profit. We received grant funding in July of 2022, and went from all-volunteer to a small paid staff. We are entering our second funded year from July 2023–July 2024.

All roles are remote. We are based in Pittsburgh, PA but will interview candidates from anywhere in the United States.

How to apply

To apply for one of these jobs, ask a question, or suggest a new role for yourself, send an email to operations@pdap.io. Please include at least one of these: resume, LinkedIn profile, personal website. If you write code, please link to your GitHub profile.

Equal Opportunity Employment

We are an equal opportunity employer, and make hiring decisions based on merit. We believe that valuing diversity and inclusiveness of as many traits as possible will help us build the strongest possible community and products. People who have been impacted by the criminal legal system are especially encouraged to apply.

Part-time Open-source Facilitator

This foundational software role serves as the steward of our open-source code repositories. You will work closely with our Executive Director, who is responsible for the overall mission and product roadmap. Your ultimate responsibility will be to ensure our software toolkit becomes more useful and maintainable as it grows.

Responsibilities

  • Review community Pull Requests to ensure our code is generally working and testable.
  • Help set standards for code quality, and implement a community code review process.
  • Maintain repository structure and contributor-facing documentation to keep things organized.
  • Refine the existing infrastructure for our Django app and web scrapers to be more contributor-friendly.
  • Contribute technical guidance to GitHub issues and feature requirements.
  • Write occasional code to fill gaps left by the community and keep our roadmap moving.

Time commitment

This is flexible based on the tasks you can take on. As our organization grows, we may expand the role to full-time or bring on additional facilitators.

Compensation

$40 per hour minimum, scaled based on experience. Our annual budget for this role is $30,000–75,000 depending on the applicant's skills, availability, and interest in writing code.

Characteristics of strong applicants

  • Demonstrated experience publishing projects made with Python; especially web scraping, Django, and notebooks like Jupyter.
  • Excited about our mission.
  • Aligned with the collaborative ethos of open-source software development.
  • Excellent written communicator.
  • Enjoys being a mentor and working with inexperienced coders.
  • Comfortable giving and receiving feedback.

Part-time software development opportunities

We are looking for skilled applicants of various experience levels to contribute code toward our development milestones. These are browser-based tools built on our Data Sources database.

Responsibilities

  • Work with the Executive Director to identify suitable projects.
  • Execute projects and submit code to our open-source repositories for approval.
  • Document and test new code.

Time commitment

These are flexible part-time engagements, lasting between 1 and 6 months. For you, this might mean an internship, fellowship, or contract gig.

Compensation

$25 per hour minimum, scaled based on experience. Our annual budget for this work is $50,000–100,000, divided among a shifting multidisciplinary team.

Characteristics of strong applicants

  • Experience using Python to create front end experiences and APIs.
  • Excited about open-source and our mission.
  • Comfortable giving and receiving feedback.
  • Willing to present incomplete work, iterate, and solve problems collaboratively.

Part-time Development Director

Our baseline team is grant-funded, but we need more individual donor support to build capacity..

Responsibilities

  • Develop and execute a strategy for fundraising with individual donors at PDAP.
  • Manage relationships and communication with existing donors.
  • Support existing funder networking and grant writing efforts.
  • Help guide the messaging and outreach about PDAP’s activities.

Time commitment

These is a steady but flexible part-time engagement depending on your experience level and availability.

Compensation

$25 per hour minimum, depending on experience. Our annual budget for this work is $15,000–20,000.

Characteristics of strong applicants

  • Proven experience raising funds for non-profit projects.
  • Excellent communication skills.
  • Have an outreach strategy which goes well beyond social media.
  • Bring a network of foundations and potential donors.
  • Strong written communication skills and grant writing experience.
  • Approach fundraising ethically and with integrity.
